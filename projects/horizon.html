Horizon Expeditions

Client/Course:

WEB 2620 Advanced CSS

Technologies:

HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap, and Sass.

Description:

I made this site for the final project in my Advanced CSS class. We were tasked with creating a business site of our choosing and creating at least six pages for the site. I chose to do a site for a high-end, luxury travel company.

Challenges:

I originally tried to code this in plain CSS, but I couldn't get the carousel for the hero image to work. I switched over to using Bootstrap and was able to get the carousel working after the switch.

Resources:

FontAwesome Icons, Google Fonts, pictures from Pexels and Pixabay.

Design Decisions:

I searched Adobe Color for an appropriate color palette. I chose a palette of blue and gold because I felt the colors would represent my "luxury" travel site well.

I then searched for fonts on Google Fonts. I knew I wanted a serif font for my headings to match my "luxury" feel, so I went with Playfair Display. I then chose one of the recommended pair fonts for Playfair Display, Montserrat from Google Fonts, for smaller headings and paragraphs.

It was important to me to maintain a clean and expansive look, so I utilized a lot of whitespace and also let elements take up a lot of width on the screen when it was available. My intent was to create a feeling of luxury and freedom, which I felt was appropriate for a travel site.

I used icons to represent the various amenities available at the vacation locations. I also utilized Bootstrap dropdown components to give visitors extra information on the amenities when the icons and icon text are click on.

One of the parts of this project that I'm most proud of are the "Highlights" sections on each of the "Packages" pages. I really like the way they turned out. I'm a big fan of using cards, and I feel like I was able to pair the icons and photos on the cards to represent the text content very well. I also utilized Bootstrap alerts for package and location information that would be considered important or time-sensitive.
