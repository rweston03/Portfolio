Ogden First

Client/Course:

WEB 2500 User Experience Design

Technologies:

HTML5, CSS3, and BootStrap.

Description:

I made this site in my User Experience Design class. I was provided with an existing site and tasked with updating the look and layout of the site. I went through every element of site design from the content audit to the creation of mood boards to programming the site from the prototype we created with Adobe XD.

We weren't required to recreate the entire site. Instead, we were tasked with choosing four pages from the existing site and implementing those. I chose to create pages for the four categories of art projects that Ogden First promotes because I thought those pages would be the most challenging and content-rich.

Challenges:

This was my first course that was predominately design-focused. As such, implementing designs that I though were unique and creative was a little challenging. Particularly when it came to the many carousels I used in my design. I used cards within carousels and it took me a bit of research time before I found a way to make sure the cards were all the same size regardless of the content they contained.

Resources:

Fontawesome Icons, Google Fonts, existing Ogden First logo, pictures from Pexels and Pixabay.

Design Decisions:

We were given the Ogden First logo, and that is where I took my base green color from. I wanted the site to look lively and creative, so I chose colors I felt evoked creativity.

For the font, I wanted something that would represent art in some way. I went with Permanent Marker from Google Fonts. However, I think if I revisit this project, I will use the Permanent Marker font more sparingly. I think it is effective, but I also feel I may have over-used it. I chose Lato for my paragraphs and smaller headings because it was recommended by Google Fonts as a pair for Permanent Marker.
